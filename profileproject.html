Before you buy Telescope READ THIS

There are four important aspects of a telescope, namely aperture, magnification, focal length, and eyepiece.

The most important aspect of any telescope is the aperture. It is the diameter of its main optical component, which can be a lens or a mirror.

A scope's aperture determines its light gathering ability (how bright the image appears) and its resolving power (how sharp the image appears)

There are two main factors that limit how much power you can use productively with a particular instrument: aperture and atmospheric conditions.

Focal length is the distance from the main lens or mirror to the image it forms.

Focal length is also a large number that you will often see printed or engraved on the front or back of the scope, usually between about 400 and 3,000 millimeters.
